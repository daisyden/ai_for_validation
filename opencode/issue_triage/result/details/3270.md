## Root Cause

DistMatrixOpsTest.test_scaled_mm fails sharding propagation for aten._scaled_mm.default because the
DTensor strategy in torch/distributed/tensor/_ops/_matrix_ops.py (scaled_mm_single_dim_strategy at
line 458 and _scaled_mm_scale_placement at line 187) does not produce scale placements compatible
with the eager _scaled_mm checker. On a 4-way xpu mesh, inputs (f8e4m3fn[16,64] sharded on dim1 and
[64,128] sharded on dim0) with fp32 scales [16,4]/[4,128] get passed through unchanged; the eager
kernel then rejects them with 'Invalid scaling configuration. ... scale_a.size()=(16,4) and
scale_b.size()=(4,128)'. This is a DTensor op-strategy gap for blockwise / MX-style scale layouts,
not XPU-specific, triggered by upstream changes to _scaled_mm scaling modes.

## Fix Approach

- Extend scaled_mm_single_dim_strategy in torch/distributed/tensor/_ops/_matrix_ops.py to handle 2-D blockwise scales (BlockWise1x128 / BlockWise128x128): compute the expected scale shape from swizzle rules and emit Shard placements that match (e.g. map contracting-dim shard of scale_b to Shard(0)), or mark the unsupported combos and raise a clean NotImplemented to let DTensor re-shard to Replicate.
- Cross-file with pytorch/pytorch distributed owners (assignee syedshahbaaz) and add xpu to the existing test_scaled_mm device list.
