## Root Cause

PT2E INT8 accuracy on shufflenet_v2_x1_0 collapses from Acc@1 66.5 to 0.09 between release/2.11 and
2.12.0+xpu while FP32 stays healthy at 69.36, confirming a regression in the XPU INT8 lowering. The
INT8 run emits two warnings absent on FP32: torch/_inductor/mkldnn_lowerings.py:763 re-wraps w_zp
via torch.tensor(<existing>, dtype=int32), and torch/_inductor/select_algorithm.py:3254 logs
'Constructing input/output tensor meta failed for Extern Choice' for the qconv extern choice —
meaning algorithm selection proceeds with empty meta and may pick a wrong-layout extern kernel. The
shared lowering torch/_inductor/mkldnn_ir.py::QConvPointWisePT2E (CPU+XPU) dispatches to
aoti_torch_xpu__qconv_pointwise_tensor; shufflenet's depthwise/grouped + channel-shuffle topology is
the most fragile shape for that path.

## Fix Approach

- Bisect upstream torch/_inductor between 2.11 and 2.12 for changes touching mkldnn_ir.py::_prepare_convolution_fusion_create, mkldnn_lowerings.py w_zp materialization (~L763), and select_algorithm.py TensorMeta.from_irnodes (~L3254).
- Add a unit test that exercises depthwise/grouped INT8 qconv on XPU.
- Also dump ONEDNN_VERBOSE to confirm whether the wrong oneDNN-graph primitive is dispatched.
- Likely shares root cause with perf issue #3491.
