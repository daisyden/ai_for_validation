## Root Cause

test_blas_alpha_beta_empty with complex dtypes calls torch.addmv(input=(2,), mat=(2,0), vec=(0,),
alpha/beta=complex). XPU routes complex addmv through addmm_complex_fallback in
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/Blas.cpp:126. In the general path at lines 199-204,
self C_r has shape [2] while AB_r = mm(A_r,B_r) has shape [2,1] (mat2 gets reshaped from vec to
(0,1) for addmv). Their addition broadcasts to shape [2,2], then
result_r.reshape_as(out_real.select(-1,0)) where out is 1-D (2,) triggers the error 'output with
shape [2] doesn't match broadcast shape [2,2]'.

## Fix Approach

- In addmm_complex_fallback (Blas.cpp:126-208), detect the addmv case (2-D mat2 collapsed from 1-D vec) or unify shapes prior to combining: squeeze the trailing singleton of AB_r/AB_i with view_as(C_r), or equivalently reshape C_r and AB_r to the output shape before computing result_r/result_i.
- Alternatively, early-return beta*self when mat1 or mat2 has a zero dimension (empty matmul), which avoids the broadcast entirely.
