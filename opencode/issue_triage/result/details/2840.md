## Root Cause

test_depthwise_conv_64bit_indexing_xpu produces 0.2% mismatched elements (max abs diff 0.00390625)
when the input is large enough to require int64 indexing. Depthwise conv on XPU dispatches through
the oneDNN convolution primitive (Convolution.cpp in torch-xpu-ops), and the 64-bit indexing path
inside oneDNN convolution produces slightly different accumulation/tile traversal than the
reference. The custom SYCL depthwise kernel at
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/DepthwiseConv2dKernels.cpp is not exercised for
this shape; the discrepancy originates in oneDNN's large-tensor conv implementation. Already labeled
skipped + dependency component: oneDNN.

## Fix Approach

- File / follow the oneDNN ticket for depthwise convolution accuracy regression under int64 offsets.
- in the interim, keep the skip in test/xpu/skip_list_common.py.
- Once oneDNN is fixed and bumped in third_party/ideep or cmake/Dependencies.cmake, remove the skip and re-enable the UT.
- Optionally, for very large shapes, force dispatch to the native SYCL depthwise kernel (DepthwiseConv2dKernels.cpp) instead of oneDNN as a short-term workaround.
