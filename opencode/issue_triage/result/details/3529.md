## Root Cause

The five failures are in the ported test/xpu/test_multiprocessing_xpu.py wrapper around upstream
test/test_multiprocessing.py and span three independent gaps: (1) test_is_shared_cuda asserts
t.xpu().is_shared()==True (test_multiprocessing_xpu.py:82) but XPU does not auto-share device
tensors via IPC like CUDA does; (2) test_cuda_bad_call's override still uses queue_get_exception
which calls .cuda() in the child and raises AssertionError("Torch not compiled with CUDA enabled")
rather than RuntimeError; (3)
test_meta_simple/test_autograd_fine_with_spawn/test_integer_parameter_serialization_cpu are flaky
mp.spawn timing/race issues unrelated to XPU. All are test-port issues internal to torch-xpu-ops
with no external dependency.

## Fix Approach

- Rewrite _test_is_shared_xpu to match XPU semantics (do not require auto-share, or call .share_memory_() first).
- Replace the queue_get_exception body in _test_cuda_bad_call with an XPU-equivalent helper that calls .xpu() in a child process after fork-after-init, or skip the test as CUDA-specific.
- For the three flaky cases add @retry/@flaky decorators or larger event timeouts.
- Add Cases: lines to Known_issue.log in the meantime.
