## Root Cause

Four timm models fail accuracy in training on BMG WSL2 (release/2.10): sebotnet33ts_256 (amp_bf16)
at stages.0.0.conv1_1x1.bn.bias.grad RMSE 2.49 vs ref 0.77; xcit_large_24_p8_224 (fp16/amp_fp16)
cosine 0.968 at patch_embed.proj.0.0.weight.grad; mixer_b16_224 (fp16) at running_var; fbnetv3_b
(bf16) at blocks.5.4.bn2.running_var RMSE 0.011 vs 0.003. All failing tensors are BN/GN parameters,
indicating the XPU BatchNorm backward reduction (welford/running-stat update) in
`third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/BatchNormKernels.cpp` loses precision in
low-precision autocast on BMG, likely because the running_mean/var accumulator is kept in bf16/fp16
instead of fp32, or uses a non-deterministic reduction order that diverges on BMG vs PVC. BMG-only;
PVC passes.

## Fix Approach

- Audit BatchNormKernels.cpp and GroupNormKernels.cpp to ensure: (1) welford `mean`/`m2` and `running_mean`/`running_var` accumulators use `acc_type<T, true>` (fp32) even under fp16/bf16 inputs.
- (2) the two-stage reduction uses a stable tree-sum rather than atomic adds that may reorder on BMG's smaller EUs.
- Compare against CUDA's Normalization.cu welford implementation.
- Secondary check: verify inductor's BN-fusion pattern on XPU doesn't downcast stats prematurely (torch/_inductor/fx_passes/fuse_attention.py and lowering for aten.native_batch_norm).
- Re-run the 4 models after the fix.
- if only fbnetv3_b/mixer remain, bump tolerances with BMG-specific multipliers in benchmarks/dynamo/common.py.
