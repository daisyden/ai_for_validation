## Root Cause

On PVC with PyTorch 2.12 RC (commit 489deee7), torchbench hf_GPT2_large amp_fp16/float16 inference
produces non-deterministic eager outputs across two consecutive runs (`eager_two_runs_differ`), so
dynamo can never establish an accuracy baseline. The failure is fp16-specific (bf16/fp32 unaffected)
and is bisected to pytorch/pytorch range f9cca30e..c90ce2bf (per chuanqi129 comment), pointing at an
oneDNN-backed fp16 op (likely the GPT2 matmul/linear or SDPA path) whose internal
scratchpad/workspace is not initialized deterministically. Owner already opened MFDNN-14998 against
oneDNN. Issue is reproduced only on certain PVC SKUs (weishi-deng could not repro on PVC 1100),
consistent with a microarchitecture/oneDNN-kernel-selection effect.

## Fix Approach

- 1) Land the oneDNN-side fix tracked under MFDNN-14998 and uprev oneDNN in torch-xpu-ops. 2) In the meantime, bisect within the f9cca30e..c90ce2bf range to identify the exact upstream commit that flipped the fp16 dispatch, and confirm whether the regression is in oneDNN matmul or SDPA primitive selection. 3) Add a regression unit test using `torch.use_deterministic_algorithms(True)` plus repeated forward of a small GPT2 fp16 block on XPU to catch eager non-determinism in CI.
