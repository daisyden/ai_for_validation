## Root Cause

Inductor latency regressed 10-30% on 60+ HF/torchbench/timm models between release/2.9 and 2.10
(T5/Bert/cait_m36_384/nfnet/cm3leon, etc.). Triage by @weishi-deng/@jianyizh attributes the bulk to
upstream Inductor changes: bf16 atomic-add fallback + reduction heuristic (fixed by
pytorch/pytorch#169257), and softmax fusion regression caused by
pytorch/pytorch@6a2bd1f4eea48c02605172fccb0f9f2be72ac73a which split a 1-pass triton_red softmax
kernel into 2-pass kernels that fail to fuse ('no shared data'). cm3leon_generate is duplicate of
#2223; cait_m36_384 also impacted by online-softmax (TORCHINDUCTOR_ONLINE_SOFTMAX) and
intel-xpu-backend-for-triton#5750.

## Fix Approach

- Cherry-pick/verify pytorch/pytorch#169257 onto release/2.10.
- For cait_m36_384 softmax fusion: revert or fix the 2-pass split fusion logic in upstream Inductor scheduler so the no-shared-data case still fuses.
- also evaluate disabling online-softmax for these patterns.
- Re-baseline 2.11 vs 2.9 per @chuanqi129 to confirm closure.
