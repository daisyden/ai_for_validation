## Root Cause

Performance gap: on BMG, INT4 GPTQ is significantly slower than RTN/AWQ. The oneDNN verbose dump
(see LifengWang comment) shows GPTQ dispatches additional matmul shapes absent in RTN -- notably
batched ones like `24x1x128:24x128x2056` and `24x1x2056:24x128` plus a `1x3072:3072x128256` (LM
head) -- implying GPTQ's exported graph does per-head KV attention as batched bf16 matmul rather
than a fused SDPA/dequant path, so _weight_int4pack_mm_xpu (LinearInt4.cpp:21) is bypassed for some
projections and small batched matmuls hit oneDNN suboptimally.

## Fix Approach

- 1) Compare the lowered FX graph for RTN vs GPTQ quantized models to identify why GPTQ emits the extra `24x1x128` batched matmuls (likely a repeat-KV / GQA rearrangement inserted by the GPTQ config path that breaks the int4 mm pattern). 2) In the torchao XPU integration, align GPTQ's quantized-linear rewrite with RTN so it also lowers to `torch.ops.aten._weight_int4pack_mm` instead of bf16 bmm. 3) If the batched shapes are from attention, ensure SDPA/decomp is applied before quantization.
- Validate by re-running the benchmark and confirming the oneDNN verbose log no longer lists the extra 24x1x... matmuls.
