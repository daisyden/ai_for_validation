## Root Cause

timm sebotnet33ts_256 fp32 training regresses ~20% perf and fails_accuracy (RMSE 0.00990 vs tol 0.01
on stem.conv2.conv.weight.grad) on PT 2.11 vs 2.10. Bisected (by @jianyizh) to upstream commit
9ec206397c1d which rewrote the SiLU Triton kernel to use std::exp instead of sigmoid/native exp.
Combined with torch-xpu-ops PR #2462, which changed the std::exp lowering in
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/ActivationSiluKernels.cpp:44 (silu_kernel) away
from the SYCL native_exp intrinsic toward the IEEE-compliant standard exp, the silu forward/backward
now uses the slower and slightly differently-rounded exp path. This both slows the kernel (~20%
training regression) and shifts gradients just enough to trip the accuracy threshold.

## Fix Approach

- Revisit torch-xpu-ops PR #2462: gate the switch from sycl::native::exp to std::exp behind a numerics flag, or restore native_exp for the silu/sigmoid lowering where CUDA uses __expf (fast-math).
- Concretely in ActivationSiluKernels.cpp:44, use sycl::native::exp(-x) inside silu (and the corresponding sigmoid_backward path) for float.
- keep std::exp only for double.
- Also coordinate with upstream to make the Inductor silu lowering reuse sigmoid (native exp) on XPU.
- Validate by rerunning benchmarks/dynamo/timm_models.py --training --float32 --only sebotnet33ts_256 on BMG and confirming perf recovery and accuracy pass.
