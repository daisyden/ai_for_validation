## Root Cause

test_unary_ufuncs_xpu.TestUnaryUfuncsXPU.test_reference_numerics_extremal/normal failures on
complex64 inputs across many unary ops (tan, tanh, acos/acosh, asin/asinh, log, log1p, log10,
tanhshrink) plus bfloat16 round_decimals. The XPU SYCL implementations of complex unary functions
(e.g. third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/UnaryComplexKernels.cpp and
UnaryGeometricKernels.cpp) handle subnormal/extremal complex values differently than the CPU/CUDA
reference, producing nan/inf or precision drift at branch-cut boundaries. Round on bfloat16 has
additional rounding-mode mismatch.

## Fix Approach

- Audit complex-domain branches in the XPU unary SYCL kernels (handle inf/nan/subnormal explicitly, mirror eager CPU promotion rules), and align bfloat16 round() with the reference.
- Either widen tolerances in skip/xfail lists with the corresponding tracking, or fix kernels op-by-op and remove from xpu_test_base skips.
