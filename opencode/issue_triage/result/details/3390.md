## Root Cause

In third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/Atomics.h the
AtomicIntegerImpl<T,4>/AtomicFPImpl CAS loops perform a non-atomic `uint32_t assumed =
*address_as_ui;` before constructing sycl::atomic_ref and entering compare_exchange_strong. The
mixed non-atomic/atomic access on the same address is technically UB in the SYCL/SPIR-V memory model
even though the CAS loop self-corrects on stale reads.

## Fix Approach

- Replace the initial raw load with `auto assumed = target.load(sycl::memory_order_relaxed);` after constructing the atomic_ref, mirroring the pattern in the user's proposed alternative.
- apply consistently across all AtomicIntegerImpl/AtomicFPImpl specializations in Atomics.h.
