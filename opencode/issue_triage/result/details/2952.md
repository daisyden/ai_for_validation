## Root Cause

BF16 accuracy regression in timm convnextv2_nano training on BMG was bisected to PyTorch commit
bc4d0bf39cc56f4ff3517d503dd80e225143580b (per comments by weishi-deng). The guilty change flipped
the Triton reduction heuristic for `triton_red_fused_convolution_native_layer_norm_permute_*` from
ReductionHint.DEFAULT to ReductionHint.INNER, causing one column of the variance result to shift
from 1.9934 to 1.9933. This last-bit drift inflates gradient RMSE on
stages.2.downsample.0.weight.grad (0.00135 -> 0.00228) only in bf16 and only on BMG; PVC and fp32
are unaffected, so it is BF16 numeric noise from the reduction tiling change rather than a SYCL op
bug.

## Fix Approach

- No torch-xpu-ops kernel change required.
- Either (a) relax the accuracy tolerance for this bf16 training model on BMG, or (b) work with the Inductor/Triton team to revert or guard the reduction-hint heuristic change in torch/_inductor/runtime/triton_heuristics.py for small-inner reductions so the permute+layer_norm kernel keeps DEFAULT hint on BMG.
- Track via the Triton-XPU side since the numerics difference comes from the reduction schedule generated by Triton on BMG.
