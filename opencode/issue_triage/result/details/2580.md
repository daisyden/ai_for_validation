## Root Cause

test_bf16_stochastic_round fails on PVC (confirmed by zxd1997066 after arlesniak verified it passes
on BMG) with greatest abs diff 0.00238 vs tolerance 3e-5 for 1/32 elements. The eager-mode
`_fp32_to_bf16_sr` in torchao draws a uniform random int for rounding-bit selection; for an unbiased
mean to within 3e-5 with 100k samples the RNG must be statistically uniform. On PVC the XPU
philox/random_ distribution kernel
(third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/Distributions*.cpp / RandpermKernel etc.)
produces a slight bias in the low bits, so one sample accumulates a >7e-4 mean offset. Only PVC is
affected; BMG passes, consistent with a per-arch subgroup-size / philox-state difference.

## Fix Approach

- Add a small repro that computes the empirical mean of `torch.randint(0, 2**16, (32, 100_000), device='xpu')` low bits on PVC vs BMG/CPU to confirm the bias.
- If confirmed, fix the random distribution kernel in third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/ (e.g.
- DistributionRandomKernel.cpp / philox counter wrap) to match CUDA semantics on PVC sub-group 16 lanes.
- Short-term: increase tolerance for the PVC-only path or add `@skipXPUIf(is_pvc)` xfail in torchao test.
- Long-term: unify XPU RNG across PVC and BMG.
