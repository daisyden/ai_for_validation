## Root Cause

Two clusters of tests are blocked. (1) test_sparse_add_cuda_{complex64,complex128,float32,float64}:
SparseCSR add dispatches through
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/sparse/xpu/SparseCsrTensorMath.cpp (add_out_sparse_csr
around line 390) but the underlying sycl kernel in SparseCsrTensorMathKernels.cpp lacks complex
dtype instantiation and some fp paths. (2)
test_triton_kernel_op_{_int_,}bsr_dense_{addmm,linear,mm}_*: these directly invoke
torch/sparse/_triton_ops.py:bsr_dense_addmm (line 1190) and _int_bsr_dense_addmm (line 1149), whose
Triton kernels and precomputed meta tables in _triton_ops_meta.py are keyed only to NVIDIA device
names (e.g. 'NVIDIA A100-SXM4-80GB' at line 965).

## Fix Approach

- Split the fix: (a) extend SparseCsrTensorMathKernels.cpp to instantiate complex64/complex128 add kernels (AT_DISPATCH_ALL_TYPES_AND_COMPLEX) and add corresponding dispatch wiring in SparseCsrTensorMath.cpp.
- (b) for the bsr/triton ops, enable the pytorch-triton-xpu backend path in torch/sparse/_triton_ops.py (bsr_dense_addmm_meta at line 760) by allowing XPU device_name lookup, then run tune_bsr_dense_addmm on PVC/BMG to populate _triton_ops_meta.py with XPU meta entries.
- Until (b) lands, skip the triton_kernel_op_bsr_* cases on XPU in skip_list_common.py.
