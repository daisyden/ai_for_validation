## Root Cause

10 fused-Adam/AdamW mixed-precision tests in test_optim_xpu.py fail with 'params, grads, exp_avgs,
and exp_avg_sqs must have same dtype, device, and layout'. The XPU fused_adam/_adamw kernels
(third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/FusedAdamKernels.cpp:21,
FusedAdamWKernels.cpp:21, and the *AmsgradKernels.cpp variants) dispatch with a single scalar_t and
require all tensor lists to share dtype, but the new upstream test_fused_mixed_precision_* cases
pass fp32 params with bf16/fp16 grads. CUDA's fused kernels accept this mixed-precision case; XPU
does not. The 3 test_basic_vitals failures are unrelated test-harness vital-signs issues (likely
upstream test infra).

## Fix Approach

- Extend the XPU fused Adam/AdamW kernels to support mixed-dtype params vs grads (template on both param_t and grad_t, mirror CUDA's FusedAdamMathFunctor mixed-precision specialization).
- Update FusedAdamUtils.h dispatch and the four FusedAdam*Kernels.cpp files.
- For test_basic_vitals, investigate upstream test_torch sync — likely separate skip.
