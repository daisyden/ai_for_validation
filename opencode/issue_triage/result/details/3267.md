## Root Cause

Two distinct test-list regressions. (1) test_fused_mixed_precision_* in test/test_optim.py (lines
2357, 2396, 2452) are decorated @onlyCUDA upstream but the XPU wrapper test/xpu/test_optim_xpu.py
instantiates them on xpu; the XPU fused Adam/AdamW kernels
(third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/FusedAdam.cpp:43-46,63-66 and
FusedAdamW.cpp:139-142,159-162) enforce identical dtype for params/grads/exp_avgs and thus raise
'params, grads, exp_avgs, and exp_avg_sqs must have same dtype, device, and layout' when the new
bf16-state hook makes exp_avg bf16 while params are fp32. (2) TestBasicVitalSigns cases in
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/test/xpu/test_torch_xpu.py:245-267 assert 'CUDA.used' in
torch.read_vitals() and are CUDA-specific (VitalsAPI deactivated upstream), so they fail on XPU-only
runs.

## Fix Approach

- For the fused Adam tests: either honor @onlyCUDA in the XPU test wrapper (filter out mixed-precision tests in test_optim_xpu.py) or, preferred, implement mixed-precision (bf16 state + fp32 params) support in XPU FusedAdam/FusedAdamW kernels by relaxing the dtype check and templating the kernels over (param_dtype, state_dtype) pairs as CUDA does.
- For the vitals tests: skip TestBasicVitalSigns::{test_basic_vitals, test_basic_vitals_read_write, test_dataloader_vitals} on xpu in skip_list_common.py (assertIn 'CUDA.used' cannot pass on XPU).
