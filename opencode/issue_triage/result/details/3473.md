## Root Cause

torch.randint on XPU produces a non-uniform distribution for very large n_max (~0.75*2^32): the
histogram-bin uniformity assertion in test_randint_distribution
(third_party/torch-xpu-ops/test/xpu/test_tensor_creation_ops_xpu.py:4310-4327) fails on XPU but
passes on CPU/CUDA. The randint sampler implemented in
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/DistributionRandomKernel.cpp likely takes raw
32-bit philox/mt19937 outputs modulo n_max, introducing modulo bias when n_max is not a power of two
near 2^32. This is a numeric/RNG quality bug in the XPU randint kernel, not a test infra issue.

## Fix Approach

- In DistributionRandomKernel.cpp, replace the raw `% range` reduction with the unbiased rejection-sampling scheme used by CUDA's randint kernel (combine two 32-bit philox draws to form a 64-bit value when range > 2^24, and reject samples in the partial bucket).
- Re-enable the test in test_tensor_creation_ops_xpu.py once the kernel passes the CPU/CUDA binning bound.
