## Root Cause

third_party/torch-xpu-ops/test/xpu/test_tensor_creation_ops_xpu.py:4310-4327 draws 1M samples from
torch.randint(0.75*2**32) and asserts each of 8 equal-width bins is within ~3·sqrt(N/8)/(N/8)
relative error; XPU exceeds this bound while CPU/CUDA pass. The XPU randint kernel in
third_party/torch-xpu-ops/src/ATen/native/xpu/sycl/DistributionRandomKernel.cpp likely samples raw
32-bit philox output and applies modulo n_max, producing visible modulo bias when n_max is not a
power of two near 2**32. This is a numeric/RNG correctness gap in the XPU kernel, not a
test-tolerance issue.

## Fix Approach

- Inspect the randint launcher in DistributionRandomKernel.cpp.
- replace the modulo-based mapping with a rejection-sampling or 64-bit-multiplication scheme (Lemire's nearly-divisionless method) for n_max > 2**31, matching the CUDA distribution kernel under aten/src/ATen/native/cuda/DistributionRandomKernel.cu.
- Add a unit test exercising n_max~0.75·2**32 to prevent regression.
- once the kernel passes, remove the skip in test_tensor_creation_ops_xpu.py.
