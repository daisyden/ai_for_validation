## Root Cause

The BartoszKokoszko investigation (2026-03-16 comment) shows this is not an XPU kernel defect but a
benchmark-harness limitation: vision_maskrcnn outputs boxes/labels/scores/masks sorted by score, and
tiny score differences between eager and inductor (e.g. 0.9975 vs 0.9973) re-order the detection
list, tripping element-wise comparison in benchmarks/dynamo/torchbench.py. The same behavior
reproduces on CUDA when box_detections_per_img>4, and pytorch/benchmarks/dynamo/torchbench.py:304
already acknowledges the flakiness.

## Fix Approach

- Upstream the IoU-based comparator draft at BartoszKokoszko/pytorch@7ac13e008a9298abd360fd7d391081cc762e77f8 into pytorch/benchmarks/dynamo so detection models are compared by spatial overlap rather than score-sorted equality.
- Alternatively, extend the existing model_specific_tolerance hook in torchbench.py for vision_maskrcnn.
- Verify parity on both XPU and CUDA after the change.
- no change required in torch-xpu-ops itself.
